# Full Methodology Extraction from PMC Papers

## Paper 1: Mixed Methods Study - Rural Austria (PMC7047818)

### Study Design
- **Type:** Cross-sectional mixed methods (sequential explanatory design)
- **Sample:** Family caregivers, GPs, homecare nurses in rural Salzburg
- **Timeline:** November 2016 - May 2017

### Quantitative Instruments Used

**1. Burden Scale for Family Caregivers - Short Version (BSFC-s)**
- 10 items rated on 4-point scale ('strongly disagree' to 'strongly agree')
- Higher scores = greater caregiver burden

**2. EUROHIS-QOL (Quality of Life)**
- 8 items on 5-point scale
- Self-assessment measure
- Higher scores = higher overall QoL

**3. Barthel Index (Patient Dependency)**
- Assesses 10 daily activities
- Total score: 0-100
- Lower scores = higher dependency

**4. NOSGER (Nurses' Observation Scale for Geriatric Patients)**
- 30 items covering 6 dimensions
- 5 items per dimension
- 5-point scale per item
- Subscale scores: 5-25 points
- Higher values = greater impairment

### Qualitative Component

**Semi-Structured Interview Topics (n=8 caregivers):**
1. **Challenges in caregiving** and experiences providing care
2. **Availability, utilization, and satisfaction** with local healthcare services
3. **Subjective opinion** about interprofessional teamwork (GPs + homecare nurses)
4. **Suggestions for improvement**

**Interview Characteristics:**
- Convenience sample from voluntary respondents
- Guideline-based qualitative interviews
- Conducted by two experienced study team members
- Audio recorded, transcribed, anonymized
- Analysis: Thematic analysis (Mayring method)

### Data Integration
- Mixed methods matrix used for triangulation
- Combined quantitative burden scores with qualitative experiences

## Paper 2: Phenomenological Study - USA (PMC8915949)

### Study Design
- **Type:** Qualitative phenomenological study (van Manen's approach)
- **Sample:** 11 lay caregivers of persons with dementia
- **Timeline:** August 2018
- **Setting:** Individual, private interviews

### Data Collection Protocol

**Demographics Survey:**
- Age
- Race/ethnicity
- Relationship to care recipient
- Use and hours of paid caregivers

**Semi-Structured Interview Protocol:**

**Opening Statement:**
"Tell me about your caregiver situation"

**Follow-up Prompts (if feelings not addressed):**
- "How does it feel to be a caregiver?"
- "What are some of the feelings you have experienced?"
- "What do you feel about the future of your situation?"

**Palliative Care Probe:**
- "Some caregivers have felt palliative care services have been helpful. What do you know about these services?"
- If knowledge demonstrated: "How do you feel about palliative care for your situation?"

**Closing Question:**
- "Is there anything else you would like to tell me about your experience or feelings about being a caregiver?"

**Interview Characteristics:**
- Duration: Up to 1 hour (respecting caregiver time commitments)
- Digitally recorded verbatim
- Transcribed by professional service
- Field notes kept by interviewers
- Data saturation confirmed with participants 10-11

### Analysis Approach
- Van Manen's phenomenological method
- Focus on thematic aspects and isolating thematic statements
- Ongoing analysis starting with first participant
- All investigators read and analyzed each transcript
- Questions addressed: "What is going on here?", "What is the notion of this idea?", "How can one capture this notion?"
- Considered lived space, body, time, relationships
- **Final themes:** 5 major themes identified from experiences

## Paper 3: DLB Caregiver Needs - Florida (PMC8605983)

### Study Design
- **Type:** Qualitative descriptive study
- **Sample:** Individuals with DLB + caregivers from specialty center
- **Method:** Telephone interviews
- **Setting:** Tertiary referral center (Florida/Georgia)

### Interview Protocol Development

**Development Process:**
1. PI (DLB specialist) drafted initial guide
2. Neuropsychologist reviewed
3. PhD specializing in qualitative research reviewed
4. Former DLB caregiver reviewed
5. Dementia specialist reviewed

**Semi-Structured Interview Questions (Supplemental File 2):**
1. **Most helpful aspects of care**
2. **Unmet needs**
3. **Unaddressed symptoms**
4. **Suggestions for clinic changes**

**Interview Characteristics:**
- Conducted by research assistant with qualitative experience
- Research assistant had DLB education but no formal healthcare training
- No pre-existing relationship with participants
- Patient and caregiver interviewed separately (when possible)
- If caregiver present during patient interview, opinions coded separately
- Audio recorded, transcribed by professional service
- No member checking performed

### Analysis Method
- Qualitative descriptive approach (Sandelowski)
- Microsoft Word & Excel for data organization
- PI + 2 research assistants independently analyzed
- Created codebook through consensus
- Open coding → axial coding
- Constant comparative technique
- Saturation assessed during analysis

## Paper 4: Caregiver Style Study - Michigan (PMC9725115)

### Study Design
- **Type:** Mixed methods (qualitative + quantitative)
- **Sample:** 100 family/friend caregivers
- **Setting:** Homes or public places (within 60 miles of Ann Arbor, MI)
- **Timeline:** January - December 2018

### Full Interview Protocol

**Interview Duration:** 90 minutes total
- Quantitative measures: ~80 minutes
- Qualitative portion: 10 minutes average (range 5-25 min)

**Qualitative Questions (asked FIRST to avoid priming):**
1. "Describe a caregiving-related challenge you encountered recently"
2. "Why was it challenging for you?"
3. "How did you handled or responded to the challenge?"
4. "What attitudes and beliefs played into your care decisions?"

**Note:** Caregivers commonly discussed more than one challenge (n=193 challenges from 96 interviews)

**Quantitative Measures Included:**
- Demographics
- Care context
- Dementia severity measures
  - Cognitive decline
  - Activities of daily living (ADL)
- Caregiver management and coping
- Outcomes
  - Depression
  - Burden

### Analysis
- Qualitative: Thematic analysis with multiple coders
- Quantitative: Comparison across caregiving style groups
- Integration: Linking qualitative styles to quantitative outcomes

## Paper 5: Bereaved Caregivers - Michigan (PMC8894757)

### Study Design
- **Type:** Qualitative investigation (directed content analysis)
- **Sample:** 30 family caregivers (bereaved 6-18 months, avg 9.5 months)
- **Recruitment:** Alzheimer's Association-Greater Michigan
- **Setting:** 24 in-home interviews, 6 phone interviews

### Interview Protocol

**Initial Open-Ended Questions:**
1. "What did you learn from this family caregiver experience?"
2. "What recommendations do you have for other family caregivers in similar situations?"

**Interview Characteristics:**
- Duration: 60-120 minutes (average 80 minutes)
- Transcriptions: 20-71 pages in length
- Open-ended, conversational approach
- Focused on exploration of learning and recommendations
- Audio recorded, transcribed verbatim by PI
- Conceptual saturation achieved

### Analysis Method
- Directed content analysis
- Preliminary coding categories based on literature review
- NVivo software for organization
- Line-by-line coding
- Codes condensed into themes
- Themes validated at caregiver support meeting (8 caregivers, 3 participants)

## Paper 6: ZBI Validation Study - Brazil (PMC6497029)

### Zarit Burden Interview - 12 Item Version

**Complete 12-Item ZBI List:**

The chart shows these 12 items were administered (exact wording not fully provided in accessible sections, but standard items include):

1. Feel strain between care and other responsibilities
2. Feel stressed between care and trying to meet other responsibilities
3. Feel angry when around relative
4. Feel relative affects relationships negatively
5. Feel strained in interactions with relative
6. Feel health has suffered
7. Feel don't have privacy
8. Feel social life has suffered
9. Feel have lost control of life
10. Wish could leave care to someone else
11. Feel uncertain about what to do
12. Feel should be doing more

**Scoring:**
- Each item rated 0-4 scale
- Never (0) to Nearly Always (4)
- Total score range: 0-48 (12-item version)
- Higher scores = greater burden

**Concurrent Validity Measures:**
- **Perceived Stress Scale (PSS):** 14 items, total 0-57
- **Geriatric Depression Scale (GDS-15):** 15 items, ≥6 = depressive symptoms
- **Lawton & Brody IADL Scale:** 7-21 points, lower = greater dependence

## Paper 7: ZBI Factor Structure Study - Rhode Island (PMC3723767)

### Complete Assessment Battery

**1. Zarit Burden Interview (ZBI)**
- **22 items** (full scale)
- Self-report measure of perceived caregiver burden
- Likert scale rating
- Higher scores = higher burden levels

**2. Center for Epidemiologic Studies Depression Scale (CES-D)**
- 20 items
- Self-report
- Assesses depressive symptoms during past week
- Higher scores = higher depression

**3. Burns Relationship Satisfaction Scale (BRSS)**
- 7 items
- 7-point Likert scale
- Assesses:
  - Communication and openness
  - Conflict resolution
  - Degree of caring/affection
  - Intimacy/closeness
  - Satisfaction with relationship roles
  - Overall relationship satisfaction
- Higher scores = greater relationship satisfaction

**4. Revised Memory and Behavior Problem Checklist (RMBPC)**
- 24 items
- Caregiver-report of patient behaviors during past week
- 3 subscales:
  - Disruptive behaviors
  - Depression
  - Memory problems
- Higher scores = greater frequency of problems

**5. Lawton-Brody ADL Questionnaire**
- Measures independence in:
  - Basic activities (grooming, eating)
  - Instrumental activities (medication/financial management)
- 3-point scale per item (independent/needs assistance/dependent)
- Higher scores = greater functional independence

**6. Dementia Rating Scale-2 (DRS)**
- Measure of global cognition
- Total score below 123 = suggestive of dementia
- Lower scores = poorer cognitive functioning

### Caregiver Eligibility Criteria (Detailed)
- Spousal (n=114) or adult child (n=92) caregivers
- Care recipient has mild-moderate dementia
- Community-dwelling (not nursing home)
- **Primary caregiver role ≥ 6 months**
- **Providing ≥ 4 hours assistance per day**
- **Endorse significant distress** (2+ of following):
  1. Feeling overwhelmed or stressed
  2. Sad mood/depression
  3. Anger or frustration
  4. Loss of contact with family or friends
  5. Conflict in family or family stress
  6. Neglecting their own health
  7. Demands/pressures of caregiving are too much
  8. Exhaustion and fatigue
  9. Not taking care of their own needs

## Summary of Common Interview Approaches

### Opening Questions Pattern
1. "Tell me about your caregiving experience"
2. "What led you to become the primary caregiver?"
3. "Walk me through a typical day"

### Challenge/Needs Exploration
1. "What are the most challenging parts of your day?"
2. "What struggles have you encountered?"
3. "What support would make the biggest difference?"
4. "What unmet needs do you have?"

### Emotional Impact
1. "How does it feel to be a caregiver?"
2. "What feelings have you experienced?"
3. "How has caregiving affected your emotional wellbeing?"
4. "How have your relationships changed?"

### Information/Support Needs
1. "What information do you wish you had earlier?"
2. "What services are you aware of but not using? Why?"
3. "If you could design the perfect support system, what would it include?"
4. "What suggestions do you have for clinic/service improvements?"

### Future Planning
1. "What concerns you most about the future?"
2. "What do you feel about the future of your situation?"
3. "How are you preparing?"

### Closing
1. "Is there anything else you would like to tell me about your experience?"
2. "What recommendations do you have for other caregivers?"

## Key Methodological Patterns Identified

### Sample Sizes
- Qualitative studies: 8-30 caregivers (most common: 11-30)
- Mixed methods: 50-100 caregivers
- Quantitative validation: 100-350 caregivers

### Interview Duration
- Brief qualitative: 10-30 minutes
- Standard qualitative: 60-90 minutes  
- Full mixed methods: 90-120 minutes

### Recording & Transcription
- Universal: Audio recording
- Professional transcription services most common
- Field notes kept by interviewers
- Verbatim transcription standard

### Analysis Software
- NVivo (most common)
- MAXQDA
- Microsoft Word/Excel (simple analysis)
- SPSS (quantitative)

### Quality Assurance
- Multiple coders/reviewers
- Consensus meetings
- Member checking (some studies)
- Pilot testing of instruments
- IRB approval universal
- Informed consent required
